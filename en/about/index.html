What Trace Is

Trace is a place-based app where information moves through people.
There is no central server. Nearby devices sync directly.
No notifications. No map.

What It Can Be Used For

Small Everyday Sharing

“These stairs get slippery.”
“This area gets crowded around this time.”
“There is a quiet bench here.”
Information for people who are there now.

Leaving Notes on Places

Stations, parks, cafes, building entrances.
A short line left for a place itself.

During Disasters or Unstable Connectivity

Even without internet, nearby devices can exchange data by passing each other.
It works fully offline.

At Events or Crowded Areas

Festivals, exhibitions, underground malls.
Not real-time, but useful as delayed local information.

A Journey of a Word

A leaves a short message
in a town they are visiting.

“Here, the breeze feels nice in the evening.”

The words make no sound.
They quietly pass to the next person,
in the moment people cross paths.

B carries the words without knowing,
and walks away from the town.

While far away,
the words stay asleep.

Days later,
B crosses paths with C,
and the words move on again.

And when C finally returns
to the first town,
the words gently appear.

“Here, the breeze feels nice in the evening.”

Words travel
at the pace of people.

What Trace Is Not For

  • Broadcasting to large audiences
  • Permanent posts
  • SNS-style interaction
  • Photos or long-form writing

Core Characteristics

  • Short text only
  • Disappears in 24-72 hours
  • No account required
  • No server
  • Anonymous

Privacy (Brief)

  • Location data stays on-device side
  • Not sent to a central server
  • No personal information collected

See Privacy Policy for details.

Trace is a quiet tool for sharing the presence of a place.
